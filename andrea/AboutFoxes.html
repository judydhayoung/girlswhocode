About Foxes

Arctic foxes are very interesting animals , because of their capability of lasting into the cold cold winter

In winter they are white to blend in with the snow, while in the summer they change to brown! The Arctic fox is found throughout the entire Arctic tundra, through Alaska, Canada, Greenland, Russia, Norway, Scandinavia, and even Iceland, where it is the only native land mammal.

this is a comparison of summer to winter

Arctic foxes generally eat any small animal they can find, including lemmings, voles, other rodents, hares, birds, eggs, fish, and carrion. They scavenge on carcasses left by larger predators such as wolves and polar bears, and in times of scarcity even eat their feces.

Polar bears, wolf packs and humans are only real predators of the adult Arctic fox, along with large birds of prey such as snowy owls, that primarily prey on the smaller and more vulnerable Arctic fox cubs.

Arctic foxes are really fascinating creatures they van be cute but aggressive

Foxes tend to be more aggressive to other animals when they try to find food in the winter as you can see it gets pretty rough

Arctic foxes have excellent sense of hearing and sense of smell which are used for detection of the prey. They can detect and catch the prey located underneath the snow. Diet of arctic fox consists of lemmings, voles, sea birds and their eggs, seal pups and fish. They also consume leftovers of polar bears.
